<p><p>Closed loop scrubbers are advanced pollution control systems used primarily on ships and industrial facilities to reduce sulfur emissions from exhaust gases. As shipping industries and other sectors seek to comply with International Maritime Organization (IMO) standards, the demand for efficient scrubber systems is scaling up. </p><p>Recent trends highlight increased investment in retrofitting existing vessels with closed loop systems, along with technological advancements that improve efficiency and ease of operation. Additionally, the market is witnessing a rise in demand for hybrid scrubber systems that can operate in both closed and open-loop modes, providing flexibility for varying operational conditions. <p><p>Closed loop scrubbers are specialized systems used to manage pollutants in maritime and industrial applications. The market can be categorized into two main types: DC closed loop drivers and AC closed loop drivers. DC closed loop drivers utilize direct current to operate scrubber components efficiently, often with a focus on energy conservation. In contrast, AC closed loop drivers use alternating current, commonly offering higher power and flexibility in operations, making them suitable for larger installations and varying operational conditions.</p></p>
