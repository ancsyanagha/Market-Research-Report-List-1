<p><p>Lyocell fabric is derived from cellulose and is known for its eco-friendly attributes. The market can be divided into two main types: staple fiber and cross-linked fiber. Staple fiber is produced in shorter strands, making it suitable for blending with other fibers in various textiles. Cross-linked fiber, on the other hand, undergoes a chemical process enhancing its structural stability and durability. Both types cater to diverse applications in clothing, home textiles, and industrial uses, appealing to sustainability-focused consumers.</p></p>
