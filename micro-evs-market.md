<p><p>Micro EVs are classified into two primary market types based on battery technology: lead-acid battery EVs and lithium-ion battery EVs. Lead-acid battery EVs are typically more affordable but have lower energy density and shorter lifespans, making them suitable for short-distance travel and urban commuting. In contrast, lithium-ion battery EVs offer higher efficiency, longer ranges, and quicker charging times, appealing to environmentally conscious consumers and enhancing the overall market growth for compact electric vehicles.</p></p>
