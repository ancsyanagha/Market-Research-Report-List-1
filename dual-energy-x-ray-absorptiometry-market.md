<p><p>The dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DXA) market is primarily segmented into two types: axial bone densitometers and peripheral bone densitometers. Axial bone densitometers are designed for measuring bone density in the central skeleton, typically the spine and hip, offering high precision for diagnosing osteoporosis. In contrast, peripheral bone densitometers measure bone density in the extremities, like the forearm or heel, and are often used for screening purposes. Both types play crucial roles in bone health assessment and osteoporosis management.</p></p>
